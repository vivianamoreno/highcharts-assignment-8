Exploring Female Legislators in Latin American Countries

By Viviana Moreno

A hot-button issue facing countries around the world is proper representation within legislative bodies and government spheres. Specifically, women tend to not seek federal positions because of a variety of issues. The data shown below explores the number of seats held by women in national parliaments in five Latin American countries including Chile, Uruguay, Argentina, Brazil, Colombia. The focus will be on trends occuring between 2000 and 2005. Then, the rates of enrollment in tertiary education will be examined because education rates are often linked to participation within male-dominated fields such as legislation and government.
